Chapter 2: Instruments and Pricing Engines

This chapter of the Cookbook explains a fundamental concept in QuantLib: the separation between the financial instrument and the pricing engine.

Key Concepts
  • Instrument: Represents the contract's terms and conditions (e.g., an option's strike price and maturity). It defines "what" to price, but doesn't know how to price itself.
  • Pricing Engine: Represents the mathematical model (e.g., Black-Scholes, Binomial Tree) used to value an instrument. It defines "how" to price and holds the necessary market data.

This powerful separation allows you to value the same instrument with different models just by switching the engine.
