Chapter 2: Instruments and Pricing Engines

This chapter of the Cookbook explains a fundamental concept in QuantLib: the separation between the financial instrument and the pricing engine.

Key Concepts
  • Instrument: Represents the contract's terms and conditions, like an option's strike price and maturity. It doesn't know how to price itself.
  • Pricing Engine: Represents the mathematical model (e.g., Black-Scholes, Binomial Tree) used to value an instrument. It holds the market data.

This separation allows you to value the same instrument with different models just by switching the engine.
